Four U.S. B‑1 bombers landed at RAF Fairford in England after Prime Minister Keir Starmer gave permission to launch defensive missions against Iran. The move is part of a U.S. strategy to deter Iranian missile attacks in the Middle East. The U.K. will also allow U.S. aircraft to use a base in the Indian Ocean. The decision has sparked debate at home and anger from U.S. President Trump, while the U.K. defends its restraint.
