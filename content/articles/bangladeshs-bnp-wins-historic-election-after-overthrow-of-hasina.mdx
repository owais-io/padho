Bangladesh held its first free election since students toppled Sheikh Hasina’s 15‑year rule. The Nationalist party (BNP) won 212 seats, ending a 20‑year absence from power. Turnout reached 59.4%, higher than previous polls, and a 68% majority approved a new charter limiting future coups. India, the U.S. and Pakistan congratulated the BNP, while Jamaat‑e‑Islami, the main opposition, claims voting irregularities. Hasina remains in exile in India.
