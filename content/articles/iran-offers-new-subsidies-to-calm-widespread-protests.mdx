The Iranian government has rolled out new subsidies to ease the sharp rise in food and living costs, hoping to pacify protests that have spread to 27 of 31 provinces. Prices may temporarily climb, but direct cash help aims to protect the poorest. Over 1,200 people have been arrested and more than 35, including children, have died. The UN and US urge Iran to allow peaceful protests, while critics say the security forces’ violence must be answered.
