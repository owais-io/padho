A small shop‑owner protest over Iran's currency crash turned into the biggest nationwide uprising in years. Protesters marched nightly, demanding the end of the theocratic government, and faced deadly force from security forces and the Basij militia. The state cut off internet and phones, but activists still shared videos of rallies and bodies via satellite. Hundreds are said dead and thousands arrested. The US and Israel have called for regime change, while the government blames outside enemies.
