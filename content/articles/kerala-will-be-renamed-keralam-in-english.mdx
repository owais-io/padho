India’s southern state of Kerala will change its official English name to Keralam, a move approved by the national cabinet to match how locals say it in Malayalam. The change is seen as a nod to the state’s heritage, which means “land of coconut trees.” The state’s assembly backed it unanimously, and it follows a trend of replacing colonial spellings like Bombay with Mumbai. The proposal will next go to Parliament for final approval.
