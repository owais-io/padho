India’s women’s team won their first World Cup, and captain Harmanpreet Kaur celebrated with a tearful lap of honour while reminding the media that a trophy is just the start. The article argues that India is the first non‑Western country to lift the title, but it needs investment from the BCCI to keep the momentum. The piece compares Australia’s Women’s Big Bash League to India’s start, urges money to trickle down to grassroots, and highlights how cricket can spark social change.
