After the murder of a pro‑democracy leader, angry mobs in Bangladesh attacked two major newspapers, burning the Daily Star and Prothom Alo offices. The violence killed 184 people this year, a sharp rise from 51 last year. The unrest shows how the interim government is struggling to control crowds before the February election. Journalists now feel unsafe and hope for a safer future is fading, though some still believe in change.
