Yara International chief Svein Tore Holsether says if the Iran war keeps going, the world’s food supply could take a big hit. The fight is cutting off the Gulf’s key fertilizer raw materials and pushing gas prices up, which makes fertilizers more expensive. Lower fertilizer means crops could drop by up to 50% in some places, hurting poor countries the most. He urges leaders to act before food prices spiral.
