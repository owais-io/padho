Heavy rains and flash floods in northern Pakistan have killed more than 860 people, destroyed thousands of homes and left millions homeless. The disaster isn’t only physical – many survivors feel shock, anxiety, grief and fear of future floods. Mental‑health support is scarce, and children are especially at risk. Experts say quick psychological help, community care and better disaster plans are needed to stop trauma from turning into lasting illness.
