Iran’s long‑term drought has pushed the country toward a “water day zero,” meaning its supply systems will stop working. In cities like Tehran, Tabriz, and Mashhad, taps have dried up and nightly water cuts have become routine. The crisis, worsened by bad water management and climate change, fed into protests over rising prices and government cuts, leading to a harsh crackdown. Iran’s water problems highlight how climate stress can destabilize governments.
