British ministers are talking about how to stop households from paying higher energy bills if the war with Iran keeps going. Oil and gas prices are already up, and bills are locked until July, when a new price cap could raise costs by about 10%, adding £160 a year. The government wants to protect families but must decide whether to give extra support or keep the price cap firm. Experts warn that a surge could erase the country's living‑standard gains.
