Nepal’s recent general election follows protests that ousted its prime minister. Voters, about 60% turnout, chose between seasoned politicians and a young anti‑corruption movement. No party won a clear majority, so coalition talks are expected. Key rivals include former PM KP Sharma Oli, ex‑rapper mayor Balendra Shah, and new Nepali Congress leader Gagan Thapa. The results will shape Nepal’s fight against corruption, job creation, and its relations with India and China.
