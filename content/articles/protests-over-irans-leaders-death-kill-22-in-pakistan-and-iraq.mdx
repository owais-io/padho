After Iran’s supreme leader, Ayatollah Khamenei, was killed by US‑Israeli airstrikes, angry crowds in Pakistan and Iraq staged pro‑Iran protests. In Karachi, demonstrators marched on the US consulate, set a small fire and were met with gunfire, killing 10 and wounding 30. Similar clashes in Gilgit‑Baltistan and Islamabad added to the death toll. In Baghdad, protesters tried to storm the US embassy; police used tear gas. The violence reflects wider unrest across the region.
