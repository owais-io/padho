India’s women are set to play South Africa in the Women’s Cricket World Cup final. After a long history of near misses and long‑standing sexism, the team has reached the top step of the sport. If they win, it would be the first time India claims the trophy and could give the women's game the respect and money it deserves. Key players like Harmanpreet Kaur and Jemimah Rodrigues could make the difference, but it will be an exhausting, high‑stakes match.
