In Los Angeles, many Iranians are cheering the U.S. and Israel’s airstrikes on Iran, calling them a “liberation.” Lawyers, activists, and community members now think the Iranian government should be overthrown and that former crown prince Reza Pahlavi could step in. While some welcome the idea, experts warn that the push for a new ruler could lead to more violence and another authoritarian rule. The debate shows deep divides in the Iranian‑American community about how to help their homeland.
