US and Israel hit Iran’s oil sites, raising worries that the Strait of Hormuz – a key route for 20% of global oil – might be shut. If ships can’t pass, prices could jump from $67 to over $100 a barrel, hurting economies and making gas pumps costlier worldwide. Analysts say the move is a big bluff that could spark a global oil surge unless tensions ease quickly.
