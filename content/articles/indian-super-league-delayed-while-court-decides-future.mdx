The 2025‑26 Indian Super League season, set to start in September, has been put on hold because a Supreme Court ruling is still pending. The league’s owners and the governing body, AIFF, cannot agree on a new contract, so the competition’s future is unclear. With clubs like Bengaluru, Chennaiyin and Odisha pausing salaries and operations, players, staff and referees are worried. A quick court decision could bring the league back, but until then uncertainty grips Indian football.
