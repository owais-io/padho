Afghan women face strict Taliban rules that limit their freedom and access to health care, especially in remote areas. Still, a local company, DKT Afghanistan, is making a difference by offering family‑planning services in ways that respect cultural and religious norms. In 2024 it helped about 70,000 people, including 40,000 who used birth‑control products. This work is estimated to have stopped nearly 300,000 unwanted pregnancies and saved over 300 mothers’ lives.
