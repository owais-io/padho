A single-tusked elephant has killed 22 people in Jharkhand, India, over several days. The animal attacks villages at night and has covered almost 30 km a day. Forest officials have tried to calm it three times, but it remains dangerous. About 100 officers and wildlife experts are searching for it, while villagers are told not to go into forests after dark. The incidents highlight growing human‑elephant clashes caused by habitat loss and crowding.
