Zoya Patel, a first‑generation Australian Indian, explains how her family’s journey from indentured labourers in Fiji to Australia was meant to escape poverty, yet she now faces renewed racism. She describes angry videos from young white Australians, violent protests, and a political leader’s comment that feel like a personal attack. From childhood bullying to her son's future, she shares her frustration and hopes that success will silence the hate, calling the racism exhausting and demanding it stop.
