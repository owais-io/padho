Iran shut down almost all internet access for 36 hours to stop protests. The block cut 90% of traffic, stopped phone service, and even jammed satellite links that many Iranians used before. Experts say the blackout is more targeted than earlier ones, letting some government sites stay online while blocking social media. The authorities may keep it on for weeks. Meanwhile, Iran’s top leader kept tweeting from abroad.
