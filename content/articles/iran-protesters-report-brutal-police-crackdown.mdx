Thousands of Iranians are still marching in cities despite a state‑run internet shutdown. Protesters chant against the leader and the former king, and claim police have fired live rounds, killing more than 100 people and detaining thousands. The government brands them as foreign‑backed rioters and has warned that anyone who helps will face the death penalty. The US and European countries have voiced support, while Iranian officials threaten retaliation.
