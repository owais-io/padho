India’s government told phone makers to ship every new device with a state‑run app called Sanchar Saathi. The company must add it in 90 days, and existing phones must get it via updates. Critics say the app lets the state track users and fear it could spy on people. Big tech like Apple says it will refuse to comply, citing privacy and security worries. The move has sparked protests from opposition parties and digital‑freedom groups.
