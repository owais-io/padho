The death of Iran’s top leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, has shaken the country. With many senior officials also killed, the regime faces mounting pressure from protests and military attacks on Gulf states. Iran’s missile strikes have hurt its ties with neighbors, and the government’s attempt to keep order is faltering. Analysts worry the regime could collapse, leading to civil unrest and an uncertain choice of new leadership.
