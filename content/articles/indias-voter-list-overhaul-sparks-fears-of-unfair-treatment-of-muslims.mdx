India’s ruling party is updating voter lists in nine states and three territories. Opponents say the move is a hidden citizenship test that could remove millions of voters, especially Muslims, from the rolls. They compare it to the Assam NRC that left many people in trouble. The BJP calls it a routine cleanup, but critics argue it will help the party win elections. The move has sparked protests and fears for democracy.
