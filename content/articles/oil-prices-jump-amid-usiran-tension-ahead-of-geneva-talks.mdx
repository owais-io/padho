Oil prices climbed to a seven‑month high because traders fear a possible conflict between the US and Iran. The rise happened just before the two sides meet in Geneva for nuclear talks. Analysts say the price hike is driven more by fear than real supply cuts. Prices dipped late but remain near Monday’s peak. The US has moved more troops into the Middle East and even pulled its embassy staff from Beirut.
