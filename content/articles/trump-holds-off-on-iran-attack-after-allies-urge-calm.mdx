For two weeks, Trump threatened to bomb Iran if the regime killed protestors. He posted messages encouraging protests and hinted at a strike. On Wednesday, he backed off, saying allies had convinced him Iran had stopped executions. The move shows Trump’s willingness to use force but also the power of diplomatic pressure. The decision was a rare instance of a president retracting a stance after regional partners urged caution, highlighting how foreign policy can shift quickly when key allies intervene.
