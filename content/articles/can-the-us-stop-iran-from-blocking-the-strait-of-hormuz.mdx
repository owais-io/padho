A rush of Iranian attacks has shut the Strait of Hormuz, cutting off over 1,000 oil and gas ships. The US has offered naval escorts, but its fleet isn’t ready and Iran is laying mines and launching drones from shore. Insurance premiums for cargo firms have risen sharply. Washington’s planners warned of the risk, yet the navy still lacks the resources to secure the narrow passage.
