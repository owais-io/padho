Senate Republicans are expected to reject a Democratic war‑powers bill that would force President Trump to seek congressional approval for the U.S. air campaign against Iran. Majority Leader John Thune says Trump is protecting national security. Democrats want the bill to end hostilities and require a vote. The measure needs 50 votes, but Democrats hold only 47 seats; one Democrat, John Fetterman, opposes it. The House has its own resolution, but the outcome looks unlikely.
