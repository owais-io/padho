In Iran, families of protest victims are turning funerals into lively celebrations with pop music and dancing to show defiance against the government. After paying large fees to recover bodies, relatives stage outdoor parties that reject the regime’s strict religious customs. Social‑media videos show women dancing and music blasting, turning mourning into a political statement. Analysts say these joyful funerals signal resistance to the Islamic Republic’s repression.
