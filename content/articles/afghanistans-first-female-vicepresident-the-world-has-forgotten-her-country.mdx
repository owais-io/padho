Sima Samar, Afghanistan’s first female vice‑president, now lives in exile and warns that the world has turned its back on her country. She says the Taliban’s return has erased the progress women made in education and politics. While wars in Gaza, Ukraine and elsewhere grab headlines, Afghanistan is silent. Samar urges Australia to keep recognising the former Afghan embassy and to help hold the Taliban accountable. Small acts of resistance matter, and international attention is still needed.
