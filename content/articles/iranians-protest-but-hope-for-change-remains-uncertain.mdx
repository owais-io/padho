In Iran, protests are back after years of quiet anger. People are frustrated that the government ignores their demands, even after street marches, online posts and university rallies. Past uprisings have been crushed, and the country’s future feels stuck. There is worry that foreign powers, especially the United States, could worsen the situation. Violence keeps rising, and many fear the regime will stay in power, making change hard to achieve.
