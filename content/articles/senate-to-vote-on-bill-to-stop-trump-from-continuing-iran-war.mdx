The U.S. Senate will vote on a bill that would require President Trump to get congressional approval before any further military action against Iran. Democrats say the resolution will stop the president’s “Iran war,” but most Republicans plan to vote against it, keeping Trump’s power to act unchecked. The vote comes amid ongoing U.S. and Israeli strikes on Iran and rising casualties, with global markets falling as uncertainty grows.
