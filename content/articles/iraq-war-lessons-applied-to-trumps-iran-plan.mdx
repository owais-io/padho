A former UK military chief writes a letter to The Guardian about how the 2003 Iraq invasion was planned. He asks at the last meeting before the war about what would happen after victory, and learns that the U.S. had everything, but he never saw the plans. He compares this to President Trump’s approach to Iran, saying the U.S. is no longer a true ally. The letter urges reflection on changing friendships and war tactics.
