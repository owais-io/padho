Pentagon officials told lawmakers that the first six days of the US fight against Iran have already cost more than $11 billion in munitions alone. But the real price is higher, adding forces, medical care and aircraft replacements. Daily munitions spend fell from $2 billion to $1 billion, and costs could rise if the conflict escalates. Congress wants clearer totals, while the Trump administration stays vague on duration and goals.
