Filmmaker Jafar Panahi, living in the US, says Iran’s Khamenei government can’t keep power after the deadly crackdown on protests that began in December. He points to over 2,500 deaths and a nationwide internet blackout as signs of a huge massacre. Panahi predicts the regime will eventually collapse, but its timing is uncertain. He cautions Western governments not to treat the regime as rational actors and notes calls for a referendum after the shah’s son returns.
