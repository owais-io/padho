The past week’s monsoon brought record‑breaking rain to Pakistan and north‑west India, sparking flash floods, landslides and hundreds of deaths. A single cloudburst in Pakistan’s Buner district dumped 150 mm in an hour, killing 240 people. Since the monsoon began, about 750 people have died. Mumbai also faced extreme flooding, killing 21, with roads and trains shut. The storms caused power outages, road blockages, and a few missing people.
