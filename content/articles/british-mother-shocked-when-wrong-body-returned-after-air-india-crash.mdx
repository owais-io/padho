A British mother discovered the wrong body had been sent back in her son’s casket after the Air India crash. The plane crashed on 12 June, killing 241 people, including 52 Britons. DNA tests first matched her son, but later tests showed the remains were not his. Families say at least two of 12 caskets were wrong, hinting that many of the 240 remains could be misidentified. They want the UK government to investigate.
