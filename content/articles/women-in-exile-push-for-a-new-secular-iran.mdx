Women in exile from Iran are leading protests in London and calling for a secular, democratic republic after decades of rule by clerics and theocracy. They hope leaders like Maryam Rajavi could take charge and that the West will back the movement. The protests follow mass unrest inside Iran, where hundreds have died and thousands are jailed. Activists warn that the regime could crack down harder if outside pressure is too strong.
