Iranian striker Mehdi Taremi scored for Olympiakos but couldn't celebrate because his country was in turmoil. In Iran, protests over high inflation and a weak currency have been met with internet shutdowns and violence. Famous players like Taremi, Ali Daei and former captain Voria Ghafouri have used football to support protestors, risking arrest and exile. Their voices show how sport can become a platform for political dissent even under a strict regime.
