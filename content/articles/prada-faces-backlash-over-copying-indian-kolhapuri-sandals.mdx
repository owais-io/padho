Prada admitted that its new leather sandals look a lot like India’s traditional Kolhapuri chappals. Indian makers and a Maharashtra chamber said the brand copied their design without credit. Critics call it cultural appropriation, pointing out that the cheap Indian sandals cost $12, while Prada sells them for $800. Prada says it will talk to local artisans about fair help. Some think the controversy could raise the sandals’ popularity in India.
