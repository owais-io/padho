Heavy rains have caused flash floods that have killed 32 people, including 16 children, in Pakistan’s Punjab and Khyber Pakhtunkhwa provinces. A family of tourists was swept away by the Swat River while waiting for help, sparking anger at the provincial government. Officials have been suspended and an inquiry started. Critics say the government failed to warn tourists and respond quickly, raising concerns about climate‑change preparedness.
