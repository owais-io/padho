After a protest‑triggered shutdown, Iranian authorities want to make the split permanent. Only people cleared by the government would get a filtered version of the world’s internet; everyone else would be stuck on a domestic network that has no links to the outside world. The plan is part of a long‑term effort to control online activity, and it could hurt Iran’s economy and cut citizens from global information.
