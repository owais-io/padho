At Old Trafford in Manchester, 20,000 fans watched Joe Root score 150 runs and lift England to 544 for 7 in the first innings of the fourth Test against India. Root’s century put him in second on the all‑time run list, behind Sachin Tendulkar. England’s bowlers, led by Ben Stokes’ five‑wicket haul, then restricted India to 319. With the scoreline and the crowd’s cheers, England now sits comfortably ahead in the series.
