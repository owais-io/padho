Stock prices fell worldwide because worries about a lasting Middle East conflict grew. Oil and gas prices jumped, making investors fear higher inflation and a delay in interest‑rate cuts. In Europe, the FTSE 100, Germany’s DAX, and France’s CAC all slipped about 1.5‑1.6%. In the U.S., the Dow dropped 2%, the S&P 500 1.3%, and the Nasdaq 1%. Oil hit almost $85 a barrel, and European gas rose over 3%. Airlines and other companies feel the hit from higher fuel costs.
