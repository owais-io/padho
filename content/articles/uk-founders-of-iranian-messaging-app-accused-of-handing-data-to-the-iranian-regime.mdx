The creators of a popular Iranian messaging app, Gap Messenger, live in a British coastal town but are linked to a UK branch of an Iranian software firm. Their company is accused by watchdogs of handing user data to the Iranian government, which uses domestic apps to monitor and silence dissent amid a crackdown on protests. The app claims encryption, but evidence shows it may share information with censorship authorities.
