Sonam Wangchuk, a well‑known environmentalist and teacher from Ladakh, was taken into custody after protests in the region’s capital, Leh, turned violent. Police fired live rounds, killing four demonstrators, and set fire to BJP offices. The central government says Wangchuk stirred the unrest, but he says he called for peaceful statehood. He has led a campaign for Ladakh’s autonomy and invented the ice‑stupa. His NGO is now barred from foreign money, part of a broader crackdown on dissent.
