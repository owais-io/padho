After 36 years of rule, Iran’s Supreme Leader Ayatollah Ali Khamenei died, sparking a split reaction across the country. In Tehran and other cities, some crowds wept and held candles, while others lit fireworks, danced, and celebrated the end of the regime. Victims of last year’s protests say his death feels like a victory. Still, many fear what will happen next, worry about possible chaos and uncertainty.
