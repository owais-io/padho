The war between the U.S. and Israel and Iran has caused a huge spike in oil prices, disrupting fuel and food supplies worldwide. Prices at gas stations are higher, and the closed Hormuz Strait cuts out a key oil route, hurting poorer countries most. Hospitals, schools, and homes in places like Sudan, Bangladesh, and Gaza face shortages and damage. Millions of refugees and the poorest are now in danger, with aid delayed and costs soaring.
