When Iran’s Supreme Leader, Ayatollah Ali Khamenei, was killed, Iranian Australians in cities like Melbourne and Sydney turned planned protests into celebrations. People gathered at Federation Square, Parliament steps, and outside the closed Iranian embassy, cheering the death and praying for a freer Iran. Community leaders say the event gives them hope, though they warn about possible retaliation and doubt whether a new government will replace the regime. They also support ex‑royal Reza Pahlavi as a potential leader.
