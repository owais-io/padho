A sudden, heavy downpour turned the town of Dharali in Uttarakhand into a muddy disaster. The flood swept away houses, left at least five dead, and left about 100 people missing, including soldiers. Rescue teams, the army, and state officials are fighting heavy rain and damaged roads to find survivors. The event highlights how extreme monsoon storms and climate change are making such floods more common in India.
