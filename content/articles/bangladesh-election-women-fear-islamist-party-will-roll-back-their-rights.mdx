Bangladesh is holding its first free election in 17 years after a violent uprising. Many voters are excited, but women are worried. The Islamist party Jamaat e‑Islami is gaining support, but it has no female candidates and its leaders say women cannot lead. Women protest that the party’s policies could reduce work hours, limit dress, and increase harassment. They fear a return to a stricter, less equal society.
