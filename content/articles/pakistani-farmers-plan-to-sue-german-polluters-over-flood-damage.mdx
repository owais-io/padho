A group of 43 Pakistani farmers from Sindh, who lost crops and homes in the 2022 floods, are threatening to sue Germany’s big polluters RWE and Heidelberg Materials. They say the companies’ emissions caused the heavy rains that flooded their land, costing them about €1 million. The farmers want the firms to admit responsibility and pay for the damage. If the companies refuse, the farmers plan to go to court in December, joining a growing trend of cross‑border climate lawsuits.
