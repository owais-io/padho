Three children have died and over 40 minors have been arrested during Iran’s eight‑day protests that began after the currency collapsed and prices rose. Human‑rights groups say police and security forces have fired indiscriminately at crowds, even when children were present. The protests are happening in at least 78 cities and have already led to 990 arrests and at least 20 deaths. The crackdown is being condemned as a violation of international law.
