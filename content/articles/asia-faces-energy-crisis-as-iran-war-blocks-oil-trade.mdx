The war between the U.S. and Iran has shut the Hormuz strait, a key oil route, causing prices to jump. Asian countries that rely on Middle‑East oil are scrambling: China and India are buying from other suppliers, Japan is releasing stockpiles, and nations like Bangladesh and Myanmar are rationing fuel and cutting prices. Governments are also reducing work hours or cutting fuel subsidies to keep energy affordable.
