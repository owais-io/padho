England lost their opening one‑day match to Sri Lanka, extending a run of 11 home defeats. The game also saw the return of opener Zak Crawley after a long absence. Fans noticed his shaky start and the team’s choice of him, which raised questions about England’s selection process. The article argues that the country’s domestic structure and the lack of regular one‑day cricket make it hard to pick reliable openers.
