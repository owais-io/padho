The US‑Israel missile attacks on Iran have cut gas flow through the Strait of Hormuz, sending global gas prices up roughly 40‑50%. This could raise Australian household and business energy bills, as the country’s gas prices are tied to the world market. The government has a gas‑reservation plan that will set aside some LNG for domestic use, but it isn’t in force yet. Experts warn that prices could rise sharply and stay high for a while.
