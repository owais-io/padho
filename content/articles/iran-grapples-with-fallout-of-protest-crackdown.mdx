After a wave of protests in Iran, security forces violently crushed demonstrations, leaving thousands dead and wounded. The government shut down the internet and banned press coverage, sparking criticism from reformists and academics. Calls are growing for an independent inquiry into the death toll, faster internet restoration, and a change in foreign‑policy stance to ease sanctions and inflation. The unrest has deepened political divides and raised doubts about the regime’s future.
