Iran’s authorities have delayed the execution of 26‑year‑old Erfan Soltani, a protester who was sentenced to death just days after joining street demonstrations. The move came after a call from prison officials, but no explanation was given. Soltani, a clothing‑shop worker, was arrested in Karaj and denied a lawyer, and his family has spent days in uncertainty. His case highlights the swift, often unfair trials Iran is giving to protestors.
