Gas prices in Detroit are climbing after the war between Israel and Iran, which has hit the oil supply. Drivers are paying up to $4.30 a gallon, up 60 cents in a week, and many are frustrated. The spike worries residents in Michigan, a swing state that helped elect Trump. Some blame the war, OPEC, and state taxes, while others feel the price rise is temporary. The surge has also hit American drivers far cheaper than in Europe.
