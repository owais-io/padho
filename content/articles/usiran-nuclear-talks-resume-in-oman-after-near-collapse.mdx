U.S. and Iranian officials almost broke off nuclear talks after the U.S. rejected Iran’s request to move the meetings from Turkey to Oman. Arab allies urged the White House to keep talks alive, and the U.S. agreed to hold them in Muscat on Friday. The talks will cover Iran’s nuclear program, but the U.S. also wants discussions on missiles, terrorism and other issues. Iran wants to keep the right to enrich uranium.
