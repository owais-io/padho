Because of fighting between the U.S. and Israel and Iran’s move to stop ships in the Strait of Hormuz, oil prices jumped. The blockade shut off about 20% of world oil traffic and made investors nervous, even though OPEC+ said it would pump more barrels. Prices are expected to hit $70‑$80 a barrel and could lift pump costs in the UK. Stocks fell and gold rose as people sought safety.
