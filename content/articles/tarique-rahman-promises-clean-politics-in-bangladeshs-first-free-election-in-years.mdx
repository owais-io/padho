Bangladesh is voting in its first free election in 17 years. Tarique Rahman, who has lived in exile for 17 years, is leading the BNP and promises to clean up corruption. Many voters hope the vote will bring real democracy after former prime minister Sheikh Hasina was toppled. Analysts say a fair election is crucial, but the rise of Islamist parties and strained ties with India could still challenge the new government.
