Pakistan's parliament rushed through a 27th constitutional amendment that makes army chief General Asim Munir the most powerful figure in the country. The change gives him control over the army, navy and air force, a 10‑year term, lifetime immunity and even authority over the nuclear arsenal. It also replaces the Supreme Court with a new court chosen by the government. Critics call it a “constitutional coup” that weakens democracy and the judiciary.
