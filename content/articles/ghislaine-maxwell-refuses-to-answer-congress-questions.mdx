Ghislaine Maxwell, serving a 20‑year prison sentence for her role in Jeffrey Epstein’s crimes, declined to speak during a closed‑door congressional hearing. U.S. House members, especially Democrat Robert Garcia, blasted her silence and called for more transparency on Epstein’s alleged network. Maxwell’s lawyer said she would stay silent because of a pending appeal and claimed she would speak if given clemency. The White House has yet to respond.
