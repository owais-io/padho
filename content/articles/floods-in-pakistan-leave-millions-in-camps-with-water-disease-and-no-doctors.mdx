Pakistan's floods left millions in camps with standing water and poor sanitation. People who lost homes are now facing rising cases of cholera, malaria and dengue. Doctors say patient numbers have doubled, but only a few mobile clinics serve the area. Women and children lack clean toilets, menstrual products, and safe water. The situation puts many at risk of disease and even violence.
