Afghan filmmaker Shahrbanoo Sadat made history with her movie “No Good Men,” the country’s first romantic comedy. The film follows a Kabul TV reporter as she finds love and laughter just before the Taliban take over again. Sadat wrote, directed, and stars in it, aiming to show everyday joy amid chaos. Despite doubts from European backers, the film opened the 76th Berlin Film Festival, proving Afghan stories can be funny, heartfelt, and bold.
