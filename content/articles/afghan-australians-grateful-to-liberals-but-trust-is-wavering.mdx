Afghan Australians have long felt grateful to the Liberal Party for helping 4,000 refugees leave Afghanistan in 2021. But a new immigration plan that would blacklist certain countries has shaken that trust. In Melbourne’s diverse suburbs, the Afghan community is thriving—opening businesses, playing cricket and holding cultural festivals with free camel rides. The article shows how the community’s gratitude is mixed with fear of being lumped together with the Taliban, while Australians still value their contributions.
