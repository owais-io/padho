Iran has started to lift parts of its internet shutdown that began in January after protests, but service remains spotty. Experts say the regime is testing a limited system, with some sites like Google or ChatGPT working only in certain provinces. The shutdown has cost the government about $36 million a day in lost business, prompting a slow easing of the blockade.
