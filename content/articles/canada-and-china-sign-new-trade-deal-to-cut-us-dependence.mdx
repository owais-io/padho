Canada’s new finance minister, Mark Carney, flew to Beijing and made a trade deal that lets China import up to 49,000 electric cars and cuts Chinese duties on Canadian canola, lobster and other foods. It also keeps some tariffs on Canadian cars. Carney wants Canada to rely less on the U.S. and more on China, but politicians back home are split. The deal shows Canada trying to balance two big powers while saying it will only cooperate on real problems.
