After protests over the economy spread across Iran, the government shut down the internet. Demonstrators angry about rising prices and a falling currency marched in all 31 provinces, even smashing a statue of Qassem Suleimani in the south. Security forces fired on crowds, killing at least 45 people, including children, and arresting thousands. President Pezeshkian urged calm, while the exiled prince called on Iranians to shout from windows. The U.S. and Europe warned against violence.
