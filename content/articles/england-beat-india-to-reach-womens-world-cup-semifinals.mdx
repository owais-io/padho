England won a nail‑biter against India in Indore, sealing a spot in the Women’s World Cup semi‑finals. The hosts chased 288, with Heather Knight scoring 100. India needed 27 runs from the last two overs but England’s bowlers, especially Linsey Smith, kept the pressure on, conceding only 4 runs in the 48th over and 14 in the final. England’s defence of the last wickets clinched a four‑run win, leaving India to still fight for a semi‑final spot against New Zealand.
