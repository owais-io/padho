The Kochi-Muziris Biennale, Kerala’s big art festival, opened for its sixth year and expects a million visitors, most of them from nearby towns. It shows 66 artists, mainly from India and Kerala, and even a few international names like Marina Abramović. Tickets are cheap – about Rs 100–200 – making it a true people’s event. The show is set in old warehouses by the backwaters, with art that grows and changes with time and the wet climate.
