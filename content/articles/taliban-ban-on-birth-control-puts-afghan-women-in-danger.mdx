Taliban's hidden ban on birth‑control has left Afghan women in danger. With clinics shutting and medicines gone, many women face unwanted pregnancies, untreated miscarriages and even violence. Stories from Kandahar, Herat and other provinces show women who have lost children, suffered abuse and can’t get medical help. Malnutrition and lack of support make births risky, and doctors warn that without contraception, the crisis will keep worsening.
