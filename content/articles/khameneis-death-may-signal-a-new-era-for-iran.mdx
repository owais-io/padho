Khamenei’s sudden death in an Israeli strike is shaking Iran’s power structure. Analysts compare it to the Berlin Wall breaking, suggesting a possible turning point. The regime faces a leadership vacuum, with his son a likely successor, but protests, heavy security crackdowns, and weakened fear of the state raise doubts about its future stability. The IRGC could step in, but overall, Iran may be moving into a new era.
