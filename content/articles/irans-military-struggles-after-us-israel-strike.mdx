After a US‑Israeli air strike that killed Iran’s top leaders, the country is struggling to respond. Washington and Jerusalem launched thousands of missiles and drones, targeting Iranian command centers and launch sites. Iran’s stock of ballistic missiles is dwindling, leaving it to use cheaper Shahed drones, which have largely missed their marks. Iran also tried to hit US warships and oil tankers, but these attacks have been limited and ineffective. The war has left Iran’s military coordination in doubt.
