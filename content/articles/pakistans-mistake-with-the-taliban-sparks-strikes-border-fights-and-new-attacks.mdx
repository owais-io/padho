Pakistan thought it could trust the Taliban after they took over Afghanistan. Instead, the country launched airstrikes and fought Afghan troops on its border. Militants from the Taliban-backed TTP now hit Pakistan hard, forcing a tough security crisis. Islamabad’s earlier hope that the Taliban would help stop terrorism backfired, showing how fragile the trust was.
