Saudi Arabia, Qatar, Turkey and Oman called on President Trump to stop planned airstrikes on Iran, saying an attack could spark a wider war in the Middle East. They blocked U.S. use of their airspace and urged a return to talks. The pressure helped Trump postpone the strike, while Iran’s foreign minister has been visiting Arab capitals to improve ties. The move also comes after recent tensions between Israel, the U.S. and Qatar.
