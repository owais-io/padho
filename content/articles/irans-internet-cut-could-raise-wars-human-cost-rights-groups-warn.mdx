When the US and Israel started bombing Iran, the Iranian government shut down most of the internet. The move left ordinary people without reliable news about where attacks hit or how to stay safe. Human rights groups say this blackout hurts civilians by blocking warnings, medical help, and family contact. While some people still get limited access, most Iranians cannot see evacuation notices or learn about damaged schools, hospitals, and homes.
