A new London exhibition called Beyond Burma highlights the 80,000 African and Indian troops who fought Japan in South‑East Asia during World War II. Their stories were largely omitted from official histories. The show displays medals, photos, and artwork that reveal the harsh jungle conditions and the vital role these soldiers played. By sharing these artifacts, the museum aims to give the overlooked forces the recognition they deserve.
