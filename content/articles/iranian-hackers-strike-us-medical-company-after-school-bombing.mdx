A group linked to Iran, called Handala, says it hacked the U.S. medical‑device maker Stryker in retaliation for a bombing at a school in Minab, Iran. The attack disrupted thousands of employees’ computers and caused Stryker’s shares to fall about 3%. Handala claims no ransomware was used but that it stole a large amount of data. Cyber‑security experts warn that more attacks are likely as the Iran‑US conflict spills into the cyber world.
