After Iran’s top leader Khamenei died, some mosques in Melbourne and Sydney held vigils. Many Iranian Australians were upset, saying the leader was a dictator who killed thousands. Premier Chris Minns called the vigils “atrocious”. Some community leaders argue the mournings are a religious duty, not a political statement. The debate shows deep divisions within Australia’s Iranian community about how to remember Khamenei. Khamenei had died at 86 after US and Israeli attacks on Iran.
