BBC Persian journalists in exile say Iran’s security services are tracking their movements and threatening relatives back home. They claim police know where they work and live, and warn family members that they could be arrested or have assets frozen if the journalists keep reporting. Some reporters have quit or taken extra safety measures after receiving death and kidnapping threats.
