Eight Iranian women football players have come to Australia on humanitarian visas after leaving their country. They face emotional loss, language barriers, and cultural adjustment, but can access free English classes, job and education help, and settlement support. Sports clubs welcome them – Cricket ACT and Melbourne Victory help them train, while a club in Victoria offers a team. Their future in Australian football is still being arranged, but they’re encouraged to pursue their careers.
