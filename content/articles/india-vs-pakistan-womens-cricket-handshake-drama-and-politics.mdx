Women’s cricket is gaining respect, but India’s match against Pakistan is clouded by politics. In men’s games, Indian players skipped handshakes and praised the army after a recent conflict. They were fined for these acts. The same tension shows up in the women’s World Cup, where a handshake would signal friendship, but politics and national pride still dominate. The story reminds us that sport can be used to fuel rivalry, not unite people.
