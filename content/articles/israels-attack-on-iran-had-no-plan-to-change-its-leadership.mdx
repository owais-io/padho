Israel’s airstrikes on Iran had no realistic plan to topple its leaders, says insiders. The hope that the attacks would spark a popular uprising turned out to be wishful thinking. Iran still holds 440 kg of enriched uranium, a key to a nuclear weapon. If the regime stays, the risk of Iran building a bomb rises. The US and Israel see securing or removing the uranium as the war’s real test.
