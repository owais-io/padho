Pakistani singer Ali Sethi is making a new album called Love Language that mixes bhangra beats, hyperpop sounds, queer anthems and Sufi poetry. He grew up in Lahore, where music helped him feel free as a queer person. His hit song Pasoori reached a billion streams, and now he wants to keep blending cultures while staying true to his roots. Sethi also faces bans in India and can’t visit there, but he keeps making music that bridges borders and celebrates.
