Australian police are looking into a complaint that an Iranian soccer team handler threatened activists at a Women’s Asian Cup match. The handler, linked to Iran’s Revolutionary Guard, is suspected of drawing a line on his throat toward the protestors. Iranian community groups are demanding stricter visa checks for anyone connected to the guard, which Australia lists as a state‑sponsored terrorist group. The incident comes after Iranian women footballers sought asylum here, prompting calls for better protection of the diaspora.
