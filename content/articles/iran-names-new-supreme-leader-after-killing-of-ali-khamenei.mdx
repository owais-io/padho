After a joint US‑Israeli strike killed Iran’s supreme leader Ali Khamenei, Iran’s Assembly of Experts said it has picked a replacement, though it hasn't named the new leader yet. The new choice is probably someone who opposes the United States, and Israel has warned it would attack anyone who takes the position. A candidate is Khamenei’s son, Mojtaba, but his appointment is still uncertain. The decision comes amid growing fighting between Israel and Iran, with each striking the other's infrastructure.
