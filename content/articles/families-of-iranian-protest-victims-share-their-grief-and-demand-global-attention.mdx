Families whose relatives were shot dead during Iran’s crackdown on protests are speaking out after a month of communication blackouts. Over 2,500 people have died, but the true number may rise. In the stories, a 23‑year‑old niece, a Kurdish father, a famed bodybuilder and a young protester all lost their lives. Their loved ones abroad learned of the deaths only after braving border checkpoints for internet, and they urge the world to hear their cries.
