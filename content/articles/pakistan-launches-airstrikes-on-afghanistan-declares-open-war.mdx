Pakistan’s defence minister said the country was at war with Afghanistan after launching airstrikes on Kabul and other Afghan cities in response to what Islamabad called “unprovoked firing.” Both sides claim heavy casualties, but numbers differ wildly. The Taliban says 55 Pakistani soldiers were killed, while Pakistan says 133 Afghan fighters died. The conflict is rooted in disputes over the Durand Line, and world leaders are urging a cease‑fire and dialogue.
