**Why people are protesting** - The Iranian money, the rial, has lost a lot of value.   - Prices for food, medicine and other goods have gone up a lot.   - Many Iranians are angry at the government for using money cuts, strict rules and high prices.   - The protests started in Tehran on Dec. 28 and spread to 186 cities. **What the government is doing** - Internet, texting and most phone networks are shut down.   - The government says it can kill or arrest anyone who protests.   - 16,000 people are held in prison.   - At least 648 protestors have died, but many reports say the number is higher, possibly around 2,000. **What the world is saying** - **United States**: President Donald Trump said he could send a 25 % tariff on all trade with countries that deal with Iran. The White House also said the president might use air strikes if he thinks it’s necessary, but he wants diplomacy first.   - **Europe**: Germany, France, Spain and Finland have called for a sharp end to the killings and for the release of prisoners. They have summoned Iran’s ambassador to show they are angry.   - **United Nations**: The UN human‑rights chief said the violence must stop. He said calling protestors “terrorists” is wrong.   - **Other countries**: Australia is telling its citizens to leave Iran right away. China and India will be hit by the tariff.   - **International business**: A new rule will add a 25 % tariff on trade with Iran for any company doing business with the United States. **What the protests mean** - Many people think the Iranian government is in trouble.   - The government may have to rely on violence to stay in power.   - If a regime uses only force, it loses its legitimacy and may fall.   **How to follow this story** - The internet is still blocked for many. You can listen to short‑wave radio, use satellite phones, or connect to phones near borders.   - Follow the news on short, easy‑to‑read updates. The situation is changing fast, so keep checking for the latest info.
