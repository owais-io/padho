UK borrowing costs climbed for a second day after reports of fighting in Iran scared investors. Oil and gas prices jumped, raising worries that inflation will rise again. Because of that, lenders are demanding higher interest on UK government bonds, and the Bank of England may keep rates high until later this year. The government’s spring statement didn't lift hopes enough to stop the rise in borrowing costs.
