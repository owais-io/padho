Prime Minister Mark Carney visited Beijing for his first state trip in eight years. He said Canada and China are forming a “new strategic partnership” to adapt to changing global conditions. The two sides signed a trade deal that cuts tariffs, lets Canada import 49,000 electric cars at lower rates, and opens talks on clean‑energy technology and fossil fuels. Carney wants to reduce Canada’s dependence on the U.S. by boosting non‑U.S. exports.
