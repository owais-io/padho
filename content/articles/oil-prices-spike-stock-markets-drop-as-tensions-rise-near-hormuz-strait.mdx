US‑Israeli attacks on Iran pushed oil prices up, with Brent crude hitting $79 a barrel, its highest in 14 months. The threat of a closed Hormuz Strait—through which a fifth of global oil flows—made investors nervous, so European stock indices fell and airline stocks were hit hard. Oil majors saw gains, while defense stocks rose. Gas and power prices in Europe climbed, and gold edged up.
