Sewage has seeped into the tap water of Indore, India’s city that’s been called the “cleanest” for years. The contamination has killed at least 10 people, possibly more, and sickened over 270 others, many in intensive care. Residents had warned authorities about foul‑smelling water for months, but no action was taken. Tests found harmful bacteria from sewage. The city is suspending officials and pledges new safety rules.
