Congress trimmed $125 million from a $15 billion program that was meant to replace lead water pipes in homes. The cut hurts states with the most lead lines, like Michigan, Illinois, Texas and New York. Democrats and water‑health groups say lead harms children’s brains and health. Republicans said the money would help fight wildfires instead. The move has sparked anger from lawmakers and activists who want full funding to keep water safe.
