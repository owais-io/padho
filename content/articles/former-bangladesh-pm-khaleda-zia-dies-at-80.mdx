Former Bangladesh PM Khaleda Zia, who served twice from 1991‑96 and 2001‑02, died at 80. She was a key rival of Sheikh Hasina and a symbol of democratic opposition. After years in prison and house arrest, she was freed in 2024 and sought medical treatment abroad. She planned to run in the 2026 elections before falling ill. She will be buried in Dhaka next week.
