India beat South Africa by 52 runs to win their first Women’s World Cup. The match in Navi Mumbai saw India’s Shafali Verma score 87 and take two wickets, while Deepti Sharma bowled the crucial deliveries that took out South Africa’s top batter Laura Wolvaardt. South Africa’s Laura Wolvaardt, who had a great tournament, was dismissed by a yorker. India’s captain Harmanpreet Kaur praised the crowd and the team’s resilience. It’s a historic moment for Indian cricket.
