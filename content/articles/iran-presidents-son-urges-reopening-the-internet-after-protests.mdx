After violent protests were crushed, Iran’s president’s son, Yousef Pezeshkian, said the government must lift the internet shutdown. He argues blocking the net only fuels anger and hides the true death toll. The blackout has hurt business and costs the country millions each day. While some officials support easing restrictions, others worry about security. The debate comes amid growing unrest and economic hardship.
