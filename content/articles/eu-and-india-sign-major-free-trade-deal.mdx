India and the EU have signed a historic free‑trade pact after nearly 20 years of talks. The deal will cut tariffs on most goods, especially industry and services, and is expected to double EU exports to India by 2032, saving European companies around €4 billion. India will lower duties on cars, wine and other products, while the EU keeps some agricultural tariffs. The agreement also comes with new security and labour mobility deals.
