Trump’s strike on Iran killed the country’s top leader and broke his pledge to stay out of foreign wars. The move shocked many of his supporters, who had campaigned on an “America‑first” promise. Some former allies, like Marjorie Taylor Greene, denounced the attack, while others, such as Trump’s RNC, defended it. Even prominent right‑wing figures like Tucker Carlson called it “disgusting.” The attack has split the conservative base and raised questions about future policy.
