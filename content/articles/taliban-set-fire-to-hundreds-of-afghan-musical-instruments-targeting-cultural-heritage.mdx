Taliban have set fire to hundreds of musical instruments in Afghanistan, claiming they cause “moral corruption”. The burnings include traditional tablas, harmoniums, keyboards and amplifiers, destroying the country’s classical music heritage. Musicians, especially women, now live in fear of violence and censorship. The Afghan National Institute of Music, now in exile, continues to fight for the right to play and preserve these traditions. The act is part of a broader crackdown on culture and freedom in the country.
