Israel’s spy service, backed by the U.S., carried out a quick 60‑second attack that killed Iran’s Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei and many of his close aides. The move followed months of gathering information from inside Iran, using local agents and U.S. tech. While the hit showed how fast intelligence can act, experts warn it could backfire, leaving the country more chaotic and making new enemies. The story shows how spying and drones are used to change a nation’s leadership.
