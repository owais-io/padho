The book is a long, 920‑page novel set in Kolkata’s famous Great Eastern Hotel during the 1940s. It follows a communist woman, her romance with an American soldier, and their son who later tells the story. The novel mixes politics, love and art, showing how the hotel and the city changed through war, independence and communist rule. The reviewer says the book paints a vivid picture of Kolkata’s past, but can feel a bit uneven at times.
