India has taken back 334 precious stones that were buried with the Buddha in a 240‑BC stupa. After the auction in Hong Kong was halted, the Indian government bought the gems in partnership with Godrej Industries. The relics, which are believed by Buddhists to carry the Buddha’s spirit, will be shown in a ceremony and kept on public display. The move ends a 127‑year absence and is seen as a cultural win for the country.
