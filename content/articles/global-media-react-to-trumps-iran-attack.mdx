When President Trump announced a strike on Iran and claimed the leader, Ayatollah Khamenei, had been killed, newspapers worldwide ran front‑page stories. Coverage ranged from criticism—labeling the attack reckless and illegal—to praise, with some papers urging Iranians to take over the country. Editors debated what the strike means for the region, the U.S., and international law, while tourists were warned about safety.
