India’s Aircraft Accident Investigation Bureau says the fuel switches that power an Air India Boeing 787 were accidentally turned off seconds after the plane left the runway, causing the engines to fail and the aircraft to crash into a residential area in Ahmedabad, killing 260 people. The pilot’s voice recorder shows confusion about the switch. The report found no emergency that required cutting engines and says no action is needed against Boeing or GE. The investigation is ongoing.
