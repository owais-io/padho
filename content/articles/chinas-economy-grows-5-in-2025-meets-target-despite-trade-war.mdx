China said its economy grew 5% last year, hitting the 5‑percent target set by Beijing. The growth came even as the U.S. kept tariffs on Chinese goods and a slump in the property market hurt consumer spending. China posted a record trade surplus of about $1.2 trillion, showing it found new markets for its exports. Analysts warn that falling house prices and weak household consumption could slow growth in the coming years.
