“A Splintering” follows Tara, one of five children from a poor Punjab farm family who dreams of escaping her dusty village for a richer life in the city. She marries a city accountant but her hunger for money turns her into a ruthless survivor. The story contrasts Tara’s ambitions with her brother Lateef’s privileged but limited freedom and her mother’s bitter resistance to patriarchy, painting a vivid portrait of gender inequality and the price of ambition in modern Pakistan.
