A US‑Israel strike on Iran could cause a sharp jump in oil and gas prices, pushing global inflation higher and slowing growth. The IMF warns that a 10% rise in energy costs for a year could add 40 basis points to inflation and cut growth by 0.1‑0.2 %. Economists say the war could raise fuel prices, hurt consumers, and force central banks to rethink rate cuts.
