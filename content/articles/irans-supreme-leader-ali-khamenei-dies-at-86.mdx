Ali Khamenei, Iran’s long‑time supreme leader, died at 86 after a massive air strike by the US and Israel. He has ruled Iran since 1989, steering a strict theocratic state and building an anti‑west coalition that includes Hezbollah, Hamas, and the Iranian Revolutionary Guard. His reign saw nuclear disputes, economic hardships, mass protests, and regional wars, all of which are highlighted in the obituary.
