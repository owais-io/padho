Keir Starmer and Narendra Modi signed a free‑trade agreement that cuts most tariffs between the UK and India, saving the UK about £4.8 bn a year and attracting £6 bn in investment. Parliament still needs to approve it. Critics say it skips protections for workers, the environment, and finance services, and India opposes a planned carbon tax. The leaders also discussed a potential investment treaty and a UK‑India effort to secure the release of jailed Sikh activist Jagtar Singh Johal.
