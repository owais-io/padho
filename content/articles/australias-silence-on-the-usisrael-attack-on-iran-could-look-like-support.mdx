The US and Israel launched airstrikes on Iran, a move that breaches the UN charter because no Security Council resolution authorises it and it isn’t a self‑defence case. Australia has stayed quiet, letting its foreign‑affairs minister hand the debate over to the US and Israel. That silence could be read as complicity, undermining Australia’s long‑standing support for international law. The article argues Australia should speak out and defend the UN rules.
