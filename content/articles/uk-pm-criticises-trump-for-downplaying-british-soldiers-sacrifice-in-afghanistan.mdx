The UK prime minister Keir Starmer condemned Donald Trump after the US president said NATO troops in Afghanistan were only on the periphery, not at front lines. Starmer called the remarks insulting and suggested Trump apologise. The comment sparked backlash from UK politicians, veterans' groups, and families of fallen soldiers. It added tension to already strained UK‑US relations, especially after Trump criticized the UK over the Chagos Islands. 3,486 NATO troops died in Afghanistan, 457 British.
